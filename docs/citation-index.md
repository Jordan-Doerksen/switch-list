# CROR Trainer — Citation Index

**Purpose:** every answer in the trainer shows the rule it's based on. This is the master list of
**every rule the trainer cites** — 130 distinct numbered rules, plus the Definitions and General
Rules sections — so you can spot-check each claim against the **January 2025 CROR** yourself.

**How to use:** as you study, when a question's citation looks shaky, find the rule below, pull it in
the rulebook, and confirm the trainer's claim. Tick the box once you've verified it. If anything's
off, that's exactly what to flag.

> Source of truth = the official CROR (and CN GOI / special instructions where noted). The trainer is
> a study aid. The indication **words** are quoted exact; the signal **lamp drawings** are reproduced
> for training (verify those against the official aspect diagrams).

## ⚠ Honest flags — references that are NOT a numbered CROR rule
- **Definitions / General Rule A,C / Speeds / Occupational Terms** → live in the **Definitions** and **General Rules** sections, not a numbered rule.
- **"Set and center"** and other GOI items → labeled in the trainer as **CN operating practice, NOT CROR** (nearest CROR is Rule 109). Correct by design.
- **"Switch-list accuracy"** (Classify-the-Cut drill) → a job skill, not a rule.
- **Signal lamp drawings** → the indication *words* are CROR-exact; the *pictures* are reproduced from study references — verify those against the official aspect diagrams if you check anything.
- **410 · 418 · 433A · 440** → named in the index (CROR indications) but **not yet drawn** in Read-the-aspect (no charted lamp form). The words are in the rulebook; the drawings are pending.
